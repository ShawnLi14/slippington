**The World Update** — maps that fight back. v1.1.0 is aimed at one problem: chases turning into circling a platform forever. The maps now break orbits with structure, objects, and time pressure.

## New in the world
- **Landmark maps**: procedural maps are now built from zones — a climbable tower with solid walls, an overhung pocket room with low entrances and a spring escape hatch, slippery ice rinks, and spring yards — connected by the familiar platform layers.
- **Springs** fling you ~2.4 jumps high: instant vertical escapes, and chasers play landing-prediction instead of follow-the-leader.
- **Ice**: you slide. Accelerating and braking on ice is gradual, so tight reversals are commitments the chaser can punish.
- **Moving platforms** patrol on a shared clock — escape routes that come and go.
- **Portals**: a linked pair connects a floor corner to a high perch across the map (2s per-player cooldown).
- **THE SLUSH IS RISING**: the final 15 seconds flood the bottom of the map with slush that slows anyone in it to a crawl — endgames climb instead of stalling out.

## New ways to play
- **Practice mode**: a bot chases you forever on a fresh map — learn the movement, springs and portals at your own pace. ESC to quit anytime, counter keeps score of how often it caught you.
- **Best of 5**: survivors bank a point per round, automatic intermissions with a running scoreboard, majority-clinch detection, and a champion screen. (Single match is still the default.)
- **Sound**: the game is no longer silent — jumps, tags, every ability, springs, portals, final-countdown ticks, win/lose stings, and an ambient loop.

## Also
- Hand-built Arena and Towers maps got springs and an ice bridge.
- Match telemetry (local only, `user://match_history.jsonl` on the host) records chase lengths and map usage so balance tuning can follow real games.
